Firstly, What is Global Warming?

Global Warming refers to the temperature of the Earth's atmosphere increasing continuously because of human activity. It is severely affecting the environment. Animals, plants and humans are all affected. Over 50% of the world's coral is already dead or severely damaged because they cannot cope with the rising temperatures. Fish also suffer mass die-offs, severely affecting their population. Humans are face many challenges. Greenhouse gases are bad for our health, Among individuals over 65, heat-related deaths have risen by 70% over the past two decades. (WHO) Air pollution also causes about 1 in 10 deaths worldwide, mostly through diseases like stroke, heart disease, lung cancer, and respiratory illnesses. (WHO)

How we calculate your emissions

Using formulas based on average statistics for petrol cars for example, we can identfy how much co2 you emit per kilometer and then simply calculate the total co2 you emit from driving in a year This is also applicable for the other parts that make up someone's carbon footprint.

Why did we choose these catagories?

The catagories that we chose to take into account were the largest contributors to someone's carbon footprint, so we can get a accurate amount of co2 they produce per year. Some of these catagories, such as diet, are also very important to other topics, such as animal cruelty in farms caused by a desire to obtain more profit. This topic also links to deforestation as lots of forest area is being cleared to make room for farms.
